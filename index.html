What you need

Three things to get started

Corded house phone
Any old slimline or trimline style with a handset. Speed-dial buttons are a great bonus for kids.
$10–20 on eBay
ATA (Analog Telephone Adapter)
An ATA is a small box that connects your old corded phone to the internet, replacing the traditional phone company wall jack. It converts your voice into internet data so you can make real calls over your home connection.
$25–50 on Amazon
VoIP.ms account
A cheap internet phone provider. Sign up, add $10 credit, and you get a real phone number.
~$1/month
Home router
The ATA plugs straight into your existing router with an ethernet cable. No extra hardware needed.

Step-by-step setup

Six steps, start to finish

1
Create a VoIP.ms account
Sign up at voip.ms, add $10 prepaid credit, and buy a phone number for the house. Pick any local area code you like.
2
Connect the hardware
Plug the phone's cord into the Phone port on the ATA. Run an ethernet cable from the ATA's LAN port into your router. Plug in power.
3
Find the adapter's IP address
Pick up the handset and dial ***1. The ATA will read an IP address aloud. Write it down.
4
Open the settings page
Type that IP address into any browser on your home network. Log in with username admin and password admin.
5
Enter your VoIP.ms credentials
In the ATA settings, enter your VoIP.ms SIP server, username, password, and phone number. Save. The adapter will reboot and register automatically.
6
Test the phone
Pick up the handset. You should hear a dial tone. Call your cell phone to test outgoing calls, then call the house number to confirm it rings.
Pro tip
Set up speed dial for easy calling
If your phone has speed-dial buttons, program them so kids can call family with one press. You can also tape a small contact card next to the phone.

FAQ

Common questions

Can kids call anyone? +
Yes, by default the phone can dial any number. If you want to restrict it, you can configure dialing rules in the ATA settings, or use a phone with only speed-dial buttons so kids can only reach people you've programmed in.
Is it hard to set up? +
Most people finish in 20–30 minutes. The trickiest step is logging into the ATA's settings page, but once you have the IP address it's straightforward.
How is the phone powered? +
The ATA supplies power through the phone line, just like a traditional landline wall jack. The corded phone itself needs no separate battery or power cord.
What if the internet goes down? +
The phone won't work without an internet connection. This is the same limitation as any VoIP service. Keep a mobile phone on hand for emergencies.
Can it call cell phones and other landlines? +
Yes, it can dial any US or Canadian number, including cell phones, other landlines, and toll-free numbers. International calling is also supported through VoIP.ms at low per-minute rates.
